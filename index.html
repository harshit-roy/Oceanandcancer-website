My Mission

Thank you for stopping by. I hope that you will leave the site much more inspired and much more informed on the vast potential of oceans to cure numerous ‘in curable’ maladies like Cancer, and the need for healthy oceans. “You can't have a healthy planet without a healthy ocean” (United Nations’ Special Envoy for the Ocean Peter Thomson).

Cancer remains one of the leading causes of death worldwide, yet it is mysterious as the cure remains elusive despite decades of research. The search for ‘golden molecule’ continues.

We know that oceans cover 71% of the earth’s surface, but did you know that 95% of the world's oceans and 99% of the ocean floor remains unexplored (NOAA). While exploring oceans is difficult and expensive, significant discoveries have been made but much more awaits to be discovered. Most of the anti-cancer drugs have been derived from terrestrial vegetation, but a significant number of marine compounds have shown potential anticancer activities against the different hallmarks of cancer. Most importantly, the biodiversity and variation of oceans ranging from huge ranges in temperature, different pressures, light and darkness provide excellent test bed imperative for a new drug development. In addition, with much of the marine ecosystem yet to be discovered, numerous novel compounds are likely to exist to provide the cure for cancer or other chronic diseases. In addition there is bigger risk that due to our activities impacting oceans a potential cure is lost before it’s discovered. Hence, a collective action is needed in both fronts – to protect the oceans and to accelerate exploration of untapped areas of marine environment to discover innovative marine anticancer compounds.

I believe that the oceans have the untapped potential and I hope to help the cause.

My Story

My love for Scuba diving and exploring oceans opened my eyes to the impact of environmental stress on the Corals and sea life. I appreciated the beauty but was saddened by the fragility of the marine ecosystem. On a personal front, having lost two grandfathers to Cancer and knowing stories of many more close friends and families I am curious about finding a cure to cancer.

My curiosity led me to understand the untapped potential of oceans. For example, the coral reefs referred to as ‘cabinets of medicine’ as being important sources of new drugs being developed to treat cancer, Alzheimer’s, and heart diseases. I continue to learn more about it through volunteering, support via fund raising and continue to raise awareness for a collective action.

I am inspired by nature and love to row, hike and climb mountains. At times, I am also overly-excited about conversations about Classics and Ancient History.
